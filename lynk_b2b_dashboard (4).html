Executive Summary

Total B2B bookings from accounts active in both years fell by 181 (5,963 → 5,782), roughly -3% on the comparable base. The picture underneath is more positive than the headline suggests.

The decline is concentrated, not broad-based: the top 5 losing accounts total -240 — more than the entire net decline — meaning the rest of the existing book is roughly flat to slightly up. Two of the five are acute hospitals (St Vincent's, St James's), alongside HSE Balseskin, pointing to a possible shift within HSE-related transport arrangements.

Meanwhile the business won 134 more net new accounts than it lost, and Virgin Media Television was regained, growing to 94 bookings in its first full year back. Recommendation: prioritise direct engagement with St Vincent's General Hospital — a single account representing 70% of the entire net decline.
